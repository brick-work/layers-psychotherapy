About

Jack Hambrick, LMFT

I've spent the last ten years studying how people change: in classrooms, in training rooms, and sitting across from the people actually doing the work. What that decade taught me is that most problems that look individual are actually relational: they live in the patterns between you and the people who matter to you. That's where I work.

My clients are adolescents and adults navigating ADHD, relationship challenges, and questions of identity and sexuality. They often come in feeling stuck, pulled in different directions, or unsure of what they want. I'm a member of Kink Awareness Professionals and the Queer and Trans Advocacy Network, and I actively specialize in LGBTQ+ affirming care.

We live our lives through stories, and the stories we tell about ourselves shape who we are. In our conversations, I ask questions and work to understand your world. My job is to help you move from what you know to what might be possible to know, and to find a position in your own life from which you can begin to shape it.

I'm not going to hand you answers, and I don't work from cues or a script. The push I give is a light one: if part of you wants something different, I might ask that part to say more, then get out of the way while it does. The direction we take comes from you. I help you hear yourself say it.

"Therapeutic conversations make it possible to see beyond what is right in front of you."

The back and forth is how we get there. I have immediate openings, and getting started is straightforward: there's less paperwork than you might expect, and if cost is a concern, I can work with you on fees. One session can be very powerful. Reach out when you're ready.
