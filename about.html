About

Jack Hambrick, LMFT

People seek therapy because they want something in their lives to be different. Oftentimes we're stuck, or pulled in different directions. We're not sure what we want, or we're not sure how to get it. If you've found your way to this page, that's already a start.

Hi, I'm Jack Hambrick. I'm a fully licensed marriage and family therapist in Columbus, Ohio, and I see clients online from all over the state. I work with adolescents and adults, couples, and families. I specialize in ADHD, anxiety, depression, and issues surrounding sexuality and identity, and I believe our everyday environment plays a significant role in how we feel.

I'm dedicated to the LGBTQ+ community, and I welcome queer, trans, kink, and nontraditional relationship experiences without assumption or judgment. There is more to every one of us than the narrow, heteronormative story our culture likes to tell. I'm a member of the Queer and Trans Advocacy Network and Kink Awareness Professionals.

My work is grounded in conversation. I'm interested in your story: how you got here, how you see yourself in the world, and what you imagine for yourself. I ask questions, listen closely, and work to understand the world as you experience it. My role is to help you move from what you already know toward what might be possible to know, and to find a position in your own life from which you can begin to shape it.

We live our lives through stories: the ones we inherit, the ones others tell about us, and the ones we come to believe about ourselves. Those stories give shape to identity, and with that shape comes a sense of what is and isn't possible. Over time a story can turn rigid. But a life is always more than one story, and the details a story leaves out are often exactly where change begins. So we'll get curious about the moments the problem wasn't in charge. When was the problem not a problem?

I'm not going to hand you answers, and I don't work from cues or a script. The push I give is a light one: if part of you wants something different, I might ask that part to say more, then get out of the way while it does. The direction we take comes from you. I help you hear yourself say it.

"Our lives are far more than one story."

With couples, I want to catch the relationship before it goes off the rails, and help find the way back when it already has. Much of that work is about expectations, the ones we put on each other without ever saying them out loud, and about power, including the ways gender can tilt it. We work to lift that weight so each partner can stand as their own person and say plainly what they think and feel. There is no single answer to what makes a relationship healthy; part of the work is deciding what healthy means for you.

At the onset, we'll work toward a shared understanding of the work ahead: a framework for what we're doing, what progress will look like, and how we'll know we're moving in the right direction. From there, I stay fluid. Sometimes one session is enough to shift a perspective; sometimes the inquiry runs deeper and longer. I welcome both, and I'll always ask if there's anything in particular you'd like to talk about this week.

Getting started is simple: I have immediate openings, my last session of the day is typically at 9pm, and there's less paperwork than you'd expect. If cost is a concern, I can adjust my fees to something doable for you. Reach out when you're ready.
